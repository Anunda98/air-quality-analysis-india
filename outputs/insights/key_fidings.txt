## 🔑 Key Insights

- PM2.5 shows the strongest correlation with AQI, making it the primary driver of air pollution.
- Several cities consistently record high AQI levels, indicating persistent environmental challenges.
- AQI distribution is right-skewed, meaning extreme pollution events occur but are less frequent.
- Yearly trends show fluctuations rather than consistent improvement, suggesting policy impact is uneven.
- Missing data patterns indicate inconsistencies in monitoring across locations and time.
